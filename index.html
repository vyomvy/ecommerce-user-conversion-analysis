Key Findings

What the data reveals

Six datasets merged into one master frame. Here are the most actionable insights from the analysis.

📉

Funnel Bottleneck

The biggest drop-off is View → Cart (78.5%), not at checkout. Product pages are the #1 conversion problem — improving listings will outperform checkout optimisation.

⚡

High Conversion Base

80.3% of registered users have placed at least one order. The acquisition funnel works — focus should shift to order frequency and basket size growth.

⚠️

Electronics Quality Risk

Electronics has ₹8.37L in high-value, low-rated orders (avg 2.76/5). 9 of 10 categories appear in this list — a broad quality-expectation mismatch.

🔁

Strong Retention

67.5% of buyers returned for a second purchase. The platform retains customers well — focus on converting the 32.5% one-time buyers.

🏙️

Untapped City Markets

1,542 cities show disproportionately high revenue from very few users. Top city generated ₹44,287 from a single customer — prime targets for outreach.

👤

Guest-First Journey

63% of users placed their first order before creating an account — purchase precedes signup in this flow. Two distinct user journeys exist.

💰

Healthy Revenue Distribution

Top 10% of customers drive 33.5% of revenue — healthier than the typical 80/20 rule. No blockbuster products either; it's a long-tail model.

🗺️

Dormant City Users

Several cities have large registered bases but very low browsing activity. Re-engagement campaigns could unlock revenue from already-acquired audiences.

Visualisations

Charts from the analysis

Every chart was produced in Python using matplotlib. Click to view full-size in a new tab.

Conversion Funnel: View → Cart → Purchase

Of 56,013 views, only 7.2% result in a purchase. The steepest drop is view → cart (78.5%), not checkout — making product pages the #1 priority.

Days from Signup to First Order

63% of users purchased before account creation. For the remaining 37%, the distribution is right-skewed with a long tail of late converters pushing the mean to ~62 days.

High Value, Low Rating — by Category

Electronics tops this chart with ₹8.37L in revenue from low-rated items (avg 2.76/5). 9 of 10 categories appear here — a platform-wide quality issue, not an isolated one.

Cities: High Users, Low Engagement

Several cities have large registered user bases but very low product views. Targeted re-engagement campaigns here could unlock revenue from an already-acquired audience.

Dataset

Six tables, one master frame

Data was loaded from Google Drive, merged with left joins, and cleaned. The events table was kept separate to avoid row explosion from many-to-many relationships.

File Key Columns Role
users.csv user_id, name, city, signup_date User demographics & geography
orders.csv order_id, user_id, order_date, status, total_amount Revenue and order-level facts
order_items.csv order_id, product_id, quantity, item_total Line-item detail per order
products.csv product_id, product_name, category, brand Product catalogue metadata
reviews.csv order_id, product_id, rating, review_date Customer satisfaction signals
events.csv user_id, product_id, event_type Behavioural funnel (view/cart/purchase)
