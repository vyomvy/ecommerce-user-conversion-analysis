Conversion Analysis

The leaky funnel

Of 56,013 product views, only 4,006 result in a purchase. Where exactly does the platform lose customers?

View
56,013 views
100%
Cart
12,035
21.5%

The critical insight: the view → cart step loses 78.5% of users, compared to only 66.7% lost at cart → purchase. This means optimising product pages — images, descriptions, pricing — will deliver far more impact than improving the checkout experience.

Business Recommendations

What to do next

Five actionable insights derived directly from the analysis, prioritised by potential impact.

🔧
Fix the product page first
With 78.5% of viewers never adding to cart, the product detail page is the single biggest revenue leak. Audit images, descriptions, pricing clarity, and social proof (ratings) for high-traffic products.
🏆
Launch a loyalty programme for the top 10%
859 customers drive 33.5% of revenue. A tiered rewards or early-access programme could increase their order frequency further and reduce churn risk from this critical segment.
⚡
Investigate Electronics quality urgently
₹8.37L in revenue from high-value, poorly-rated Electronics orders (avg 2.76/5) is a returns and churn time-bomb. Quality checks, better product descriptions, or supplier review are needed.
📧
Re-engage dormant city users
Multiple cities have large user bases with below-average browsing. Email or push re-engagement campaigns targeting these users could convert an already-acquired audience at low acquisition cost.
📍
Target high-revenue, low-density cities
1,542 cities show single buyers spending ₹40K+. These high-value individuals in smaller cities are an untapped market — geo-targeted campaigns could find more buyers with similar profiles.
